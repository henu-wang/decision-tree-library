# Decision Tree Library

A curated library of pre-built decision trees for common personal, professional, and business scenarios. This repository provides ready-to-use decision frameworks that help you navigate complex choices systematically rather than relying on intuition alone.

## Why Decision Trees?

Decision trees are one of the most intuitive and effective tools for structured decision-making. They break complex decisions into a series of simpler, sequential choices, making it easier to evaluate options, consider consequences, and arrive at well-reasoned conclusions.

Unlike informal pros-and-cons lists, decision trees force you to think through the logical structure of a decision, identify the key factors that matter, and consider how different variables interact. This structured approach consistently leads to better outcomes than unstructured deliberation.

## Library Categories

### Career Decisions
- **Job Offer Evaluation Tree**: Compare multiple job offers across compensation, growth potential, culture fit, location, and work-life balance
- **Career Change Assessment**: Determine whether switching careers makes sense given your current situation, financial reserves, and market conditions
- **Freelance vs Employment**: Evaluate whether freelancing or traditional employment better fits your risk tolerance, financial needs, and lifestyle preferences
- **Promotion Negotiation**: Navigate the decision of when and how to negotiate for a promotion based on your tenure, performance, and market value

### Business Decisions
- **Market Entry Analysis**: Determine whether to enter a new market based on size, competition, regulatory environment, and resource requirements
- **Build vs Buy**: Decide whether to develop a capability internally or acquire it through partnerships, licensing, or acquisition
- **Pricing Strategy Selection**: Choose among penetration, skimming, value-based, and competitive pricing strategies based on your market position and product characteristics
- **Hiring Decision Framework**: Evaluate candidates systematically across skills, experience, cultural fit, and growth potential

### Financial Decisions
- **Investment Allocation**: Determine appropriate asset allocation based on time horizon, risk tolerance, and financial goals
- **Large Purchase Evaluation**: Decide whether a major purchase (home, vehicle, equipment) is financially sound given your current situation
- **Debt Repayment Strategy**: Choose between avalanche, snowball, and hybrid approaches to debt repayment

### Personal Decisions
- **Relocation Assessment**: Evaluate whether moving to a new city makes sense across career, lifestyle, social, and financial dimensions
- **Education Investment**: Determine whether pursuing additional education will generate sufficient returns to justify the cost and time investment

## How to Use These Decision Trees

Each decision tree in this library follows a consistent format:

1. **Context Description**: When this decision tree applies and any prerequisites or assumptions
2. **Key Decision Nodes**: The critical questions you need to answer at each stage
3. **Information Requirements**: What data you need to gather before proceeding through the tree
4. **Outcome Descriptions**: What each terminal node means and what actions to take
5. **Confidence Calibration**: How to assess your confidence in the inputs and adjust accordingly

## Building Your Own Decision Trees

While this library covers many common scenarios, you will inevitably face unique decisions that require custom frameworks. The principles for building effective decision trees include starting with the most important discriminating factor, keeping branches mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive, and limiting tree depth to maintain usability.

## Decision Quality Over Outcome Quality

An important principle underlying this entire library is the distinction between decision quality and outcome quality. A good decision can still lead to a bad outcome due to factors beyond your control, and a bad decision can sometimes produce a good outcome through luck. The goal of using decision trees is to consistently make high-quality decisions, which over time will produce the best possible outcomes.

Tracking your decisions and their outcomes is essential for improving your decision-making over time. Platforms like [KeepRule](https://keeprule.com) provide the tools to log decisions, record outcomes, and analyze patterns in your decision-making process.
